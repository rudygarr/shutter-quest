Aperture — The Bokeh Button

Phase 2: Exposure Triangle 🤖 Together Coder (Llama 3.3 70B) 150 XP

Objective

Master aperture (f-stops), depth of field, bokeh, and how aperture controls both light AND creative look.

What You'll Learn

  • f-stop = focal length / aperture diameter (it's a fraction!)
  • SMALL number = BIG hole = MORE light = SHALLOW depth of field
  • LARGE number = SMALL hole = LESS light = DEEP depth of field
  • f/1.8 = dreamy portraits, f/16 = sharp landscapes
  • Aperture Priority (Av) mode — you pick f-stop, camera picks shutter
  • Sweet spot: f/8-f/11 for maximum sharpness

The Aperture Trade-offs

  • Wide (f/1.4-f/2.8): Dreamy backgrounds, low light hero, but razor-thin focus plane
  • Middle (f/4-f/5.6): Balanced — subject pops, context visible
  • Narrow (f/8-f/16): Everything sharp, but needs light or tripod
  • Diffraction warning: Past f/11, diffraction softens image slightly

Aperture Priority (Av) — Your New Best Friend

  1. Turn mode dial to Av
  2. Main dial = change f-stop
  3. Camera auto-sets shutter speed
  4. Watch shutter: if < 1/125, bump ISO or open aperture
🎯 Challenge

Portrait at f/1.8, landscape at f/11. Same subject distance. Compare: background blur, subject separation, overall feel. Shoot 3 portraits (f/1.8, f/4, f/8) and 3 landscapes (f/4, f/8, f/11).
