Shutter — Freeze vs Blur

Phase 2: Exposure Triangle ⚡ Together Coder (Llama 3.3 70B) 150 XP

Objective

Master shutter speed to freeze action, create artistic motion blur, and master the panning technique.

What You'll Learn

  • Shutter speed = duration sensor is exposed to light
  • Fast (1/500+) = freeze motion, needs light
  • Slow (1/30 and below) = motion blur, less light needed
  • Panning = follow subject, slow shutter, sharp subject + blurred background
  • Shutter Priority (Tv) mode — you pick speed, camera picks aperture
  • Reciprocal rule: shutter ≥ 1/focal length for handheld

The Trade-offs

  • Fast (≥1/500): Freeze everything, needs wide aperture/high ISO
  • Medium (1/125-1/250): Everyday safe zone, slight blur on fast motion
  • Slow (≤1/60): Motion blur, needs tripod or panning technique
  • Bulb mode: Hold shutter open as long as you want (fireworks, stars)

🎯 Panning Technique — The Magic Trick

  • Shutter: 1/30 to 1/60
  • Mode: Shutter Priority (Tv)
  • Focus: AI Servo + Zone AF
  • Stance: Feet planted, rotate at waist
  • Track subject BEFORE pressing shutter
  • Follow through AFTER shutter closes
  • Result: Sharp subject, streaked background = SPEED
