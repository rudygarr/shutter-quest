Rule of Thirds — The Grid That Works

Phase 5: Composition & Story ✍️ Creative (MiniMax/Qwen) 100 XP

Objective

Master the 3x3 grid. Place subjects on intersections for instant visual tension. Turn on grid in R7 and never center again (unless you mean it).

📐 The 3x3 Grid

4 power points = where lines intersect. Place eyes, horizon, subject here.

Why It Works

  • Center = static, predictable, "snapshot"
  • Thirds = dynamic, breathing room, eye travels
  • Intersections = natural focal points for human eye
  • Horizon on top/bottom line = emphasis choice

📐 Quick Rules

  • 👁️Portraits: Eyes on top horizontal line, inner eye on intersection
  • 🏞️Landscapes: Horizon on top line (sky drama) or bottom line (foreground drama)
  • 🏃Action: Subject on left third, moving INTO frame (right third space)
  • 🌅Sunsets: Horizon on bottom third, sky takes 2/3
  • 📱R7 Grid: Menu → Grid display → 3x3 → On

When to Break It

  • Symmetry: Perfect reflection, architecture, centered face = power
  • Minimalism: Tiny subject, vast negative space = isolation
  • Impact: Dead center = confrontation, intensity, "look at me"
  • Rule of Odds: 3, 5, 7 subjects more pleasing than even numbers

R7 Grid Setup

  1. Menu (wrench icon) → Grid display
  2. 3x3 grid → On
  3. Display level → On (helps horizons)
  4. Grid color: White (easiest to see)
🎯 Challenge

5 photos using rule of thirds grid. Turn grid ON. Shoot: (1) Portrait with eye on intersection, (2) Landscape with horizon on bottom third, (3) Action with subject on left third moving right, (4) Sunset with horizon on top third, (5) Your choice — but NO centered subjects.
