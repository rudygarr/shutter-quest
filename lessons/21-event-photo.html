Event Photography — The Chaos Protocol

Phase 5: Composition & Story 🤖 Together Coder (Llama 3.3 70B) 200 XP

Objective

Survive and thrive in live event chaos. Anticipate, position, capture the moments that matter. No re-takes.

Why Events Are Different

  • No second chances — moments happen once
  • Lighting changes constantly
  • People move unpredictably
  • You need: anticipation, positioning, speed, backup

🎯 The Event Protocol — Before/During/After

BEFORE (The Prep)

  1. Scout venue: light, angles, power outlets, WiFi
  2. Shot list: must-haves (VIPs, key moments, details, groups)
  3. Gear check: 2 bodies, 3 lenses (wide, normal, tele), 4 batteries, 4 cards, flash, diffuser
  4. Settings baseline: Av mode, Auto ISO 6400 max, 1/250 min, AI Servo, Eye AF
  5. Business cards, contract, timeline, contact list

DURING (The Chaos)

  1. Arrive 60min early — test shots, white balance, flash check
  2. Positioning: 45° to action, not behind it. Move constantly.
  3. Antenna up: listen for laughter, applause, music cues = moments coming
  4. Burst mode: hold for peak action, release for buffer
  5. Dual cards: RAW slot 1, JPEG slot 2 (instant backup)
  6. Battery swap at 30% — never die mid-event
  7. Cull in-camera between sessions (rate 1-5 stars)

AFTER (The Delivery)

  1. Import: Lightroom/DPP → cull 50% → rate selects
  2. Quick edit: exposure, WB, crop, noise reduction (batch)
  3. Export: JPGs (web) + full res (archive)
  4. Delivery: Gallery link (Pixieset, SmugMug, Google Drive) within 48hrs
  5. Invoice + thank you + request review

The 5 Shot Types Every Event Needs

  • Wide/Establishing: Venue, crowd, atmosphere (24mm)
  • Key Moments: Speeches, first dance, awards, cake (50-85mm)
  • Candid/Emotion: Laughter, tears, hugs, reactions (85-135mm)
  • Details: Rings, programs, decor, food, signage (100mm macro)
  • Groups: Organized, well-lit, everyone visible (35-50mm)

Positioning Secrets

  • 45° angle to stage/action — shows depth, not flat
  • Don't stand with other photographers — find unique angle
  • Anticipate: where will they look? Where will they move?
  • Be invisible: black clothing, quiet shutter, no chimping
  • Exit routes: know how to move without blocking views

Crisis Management

  • Flash dies: Bounce off wall, or go wide + high ISO
  • Battery dies: Swap body (that's why you have 2)
  • Card full: Swap card (that's why you have 4)
  • Flash recycle slow: Lower power, higher ISO, wider aperture
  • Subject blinks: Burst mode (3-5 shots), one will be open
🎯 Challenge

Simulated event: candid, group, detail, wide, emotion. Set up mini-event (family dinner, school play, sports practice). Shoot: 1 wide establishing, 1 candid emotion, 1 group, 1 detail, 1 wide. 5 shots total. Cull to 3 best.
