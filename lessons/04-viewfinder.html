EVF vs LCD — Pick Your Window

Phase 1: Hold This Thing 🤖 Together Coder (Llama 3.3 70B) 100 XP

Objective

Understand when to use the Electronic Viewfinder (EVF) vs the rear LCD, set your diopter, and use touch focus effectively.

What You'll Learn

  • EVF vs LCD: pros/cons for different situations
  • Diopter adjustment — customize the EVF for YOUR eyes
  • Touch & drag AF on the LCD
  • Eye sensor behavior (auto-switch EVF/LCD)
  • Bright sunlight vs dark gym strategy

🔭 EVF (Electronic Viewfinder)

  • Bright sun: No glare, see exposure preview
  • Stability: Third point of contact (face)
  • Exposure WYSIWYG: See exact brightness before shot
  • Focus peaking: MF + peaking visible
  • Battery: Uses more power

📱 LCD (Rear Screen)

  • Odd angles: Low, high, selfie, tripod
  • Touch focus: Tap to focus + shoot
  • Group shots: Everyone sees framing
  • Video: Better for recording
  • Glare: Struggles in bright sun

Diopter Adjustment — Do This Once

  1. Take off glasses/contacts (if you wear them)
  2. Look through EVF at a plain wall or text
  3. Turn diopter dial (next to EVF) until text is razor sharp
  4. Lock it mentally — don't touch again unless vision changes

Pro tip: If you share the camera, remember to readjust!

Touch & Drag AF (R7 Feature)

With Face/Eye AF on, tap the LCD on your subject's eye → camera locks on. Drag finger to move focus point. Works in both EVF + LCD modes.

🎯 Challenge

5 shots EVF only, 5 shots LCD only. Same subjects, same settings. Compare: sharpness, exposure accuracy, comfort. Note which felt better for each scenario.
