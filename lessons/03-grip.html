Hold Me Like You Mean It

Phase 1: Hold This Thing 🤖 Together Coder (Llama 3.3 70B) 100 XP

Objective

Master proper grip, stance, breathing, and shutter technique for sharp, shake-free photos.

Proper vs Poor Camera Grip & Stance

What You'll Learn

  • Two-handed grip: right hand on grip, left hand under lens
  • Elbows tucked into ribs (not flared)
  • Feet shoulder-width, one slightly forward
  • Breath control: inhale, exhale halfway, hold, press
  • Roll the shutter — don't jab it
  • Portrait vs Landscape orientation grip
🎯 Challenge

10 sharp photos of a stationary object. Use Auto mode. Check each at 100% zoom (playback + zoom lever). If any are soft, adjust grip and retry. Goal: 10/10 tack-sharp.

The Proper Grip (Step by Step)

  1. Right hand: Fingers wrap around grip naturally, index finger on shutter, thumb on back dial
  2. Left hand: Palm up, supports lens from underneath, fingers adjust zoom/focus ring
  3. Elbows: Tucked into ribs — pretend you're holding a sandwich
  4. Face: Press eye firmly to EVF (blocks stray light, adds stability)
  5. Stance: Feet shoulder-width, weight balanced, one foot slightly forward
  6. Breathing: Inhale → Exhale halfway → Hold → Squeeze shutter → Exhale
  7. Shutter press: Roll finger smoothly. Never jab.
