Storytelling — The 3-Shot Sequence

Phase 5: Composition & Story ✍️ Creative (MiniMax/Qwen) 150 XP

Objective

Tell a complete story in 3 frames: Wide (where), Medium (who), Tight (what/feeling). The filmmaker's secret weapon.

1

WIDE — "Where"

Establishing shot. Environment, context, location. 16-35mm. Answer: Where are we?

2

MEDIUM — "Who"

Subject in context. Relationship to environment. 35-50mm. Answer: Who is this about?

3

TIGHT — "What/Feeling"

Detail, emotion, action. The "money shot." 85-200mm. Answer: What's happening? How does it feel?

Why 3 Shots Work

  • 1 shot = moment, 3 shots = story
  • Wide = context, Medium = connection, Tight = emotion
  • Viewer's brain fills gaps → engagement
  • Works for: sports, events, portraits, documentary, social media

How to Shoot a Sequence

  1. Wide first: Set the scene, get environment + subject
  2. Move in: Medium shot — subject + context
  3. Get tight: Details, faces, hands, eyes, action
  4. Shoot in bursts: 3-5 frames per step
  5. Vary angle: Low, high, side, behind

Sequence Types

  • Action Sequence: Windup → Contact → Follow-through
  • Emotion Sequence: Anticipation → Reaction → Aftermath
  • Process Sequence: Start → Middle → Done
  • Reveal Sequence: Closed → Opening → Revealed
  • Contrast Sequence: Before → During → After

Sequencing for Social Media

  • Instagram Carousel: Wide → Medium → Tight (swipe story)
  • Reels/TikTok: 3 clips, 1 sec each, music sync
  • Story highlight: 3 photos + text overlay
🎯 Challenge

Wide → Medium → Tight story. Pick ONE subject/activity. Shoot 3-shot sequence: (1) Wide establishing, (2) Medium context, (3) Tight detail/emotion. Upload as set with captions.
